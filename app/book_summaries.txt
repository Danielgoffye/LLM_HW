## Title: 1984
In a dystopian society under constant surveillance by Big Brother, Winston Smith secretly rebels against the totalitarian regime. He seeks truth and personal freedom in a world where independent thought is punished. Orwell’s novel explores the dangers of propaganda, censorship, and psychological control.

## Title: The Hobbit
Bilbo Baggins, a simple hobbit with no taste for adventure, is thrust into a quest to help dwarves reclaim their homeland from the dragon Smaug. Along the way, he faces trolls, goblins, and other dangers, while discovering inner strength and resourcefulness he never imagined.

## Title: To Kill a Mockingbird
Set in the racially charged American South, young Scout Finch observes the injustices of society as her father, Atticus, defends a Black man falsely accused of rape. The novel explores themes of moral growth, empathy, and standing up for what is right.

## Title: Brave New World
In a futuristic world driven by consumerism and technological control, people are engineered for their roles in society and kept docile with pleasure-inducing drugs. Bernard Marx begins to feel disconnected, leading him to question the cost of stability and uniformity.

## Title: Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone
Eleven-year-old Harry learns he is a wizard and attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. There, he discovers friendship, magic, and a hidden world where his past and future are intertwined with the dark lord Voldemort.

## Title: The Great Gatsby
Narrated by Nick Carraway, this novel portrays the life of Jay Gatsby, a wealthy man obsessed with rekindling a past romance with Daisy Buchanan. Set in the 1920s, the story examines illusion, decadence, and the American Dream’s hollowness.

## Title: The Catcher in the Rye
Holden Caulfield, a disenchanted teenager, escapes from prep school and roams New York City. Battling loneliness and confusion, he struggles with the hypocrisy of adulthood and seeks authenticity in a phony world.

## Title: The Lord of the Rings
Frodo Baggins inherits a powerful ring that must be destroyed to prevent a dark lord from conquering Middle-earth. He embarks on a perilous journey with allies of various races, confronting evil and discovering the strength of friendship and courage.

## Title: Animal Farm
A group of farm animals revolt against their human owner to establish a society where all animals are equal. However, the pigs slowly seize power, revealing the corruption of ideals and the ease with which tyranny can arise under false promises.

## Title: Fahrenheit 451
In a future where books are banned and critical thinking is discouraged, fireman Guy Montag begins to question the purpose of his job. As he discovers literature and independent thought, he risks everything to challenge a society ruled by ignorance.

## Title: Jane Eyre
Jane Eyre, an orphan raised in hardship, becomes a governess and falls in love with her employer, Mr. Rochester. As she uncovers his dark secret, she must choose between her principles and her heart, asserting her independence and moral strength.

## Title: Crime and Punishment
Raskolnikov, a poor student in St. Petersburg, commits a murder he believes is justified by his intellect. Haunted by guilt and paranoia, he undergoes a psychological and spiritual transformation, grappling with morality, justice, and redemption.

## Title: The Alchemist
Santiago, a young Andalusian shepherd, dreams of finding a treasure hidden near the pyramids of Egypt. Guided by omens and encounters, his journey becomes one of self-discovery, spiritual awakening, and the pursuit of one's true purpose.

## Title: Pride and Prejudice
Elizabeth Bennet navigates issues of class, reputation, and love in Georgian England. Her witty exchanges with the aloof Mr. Darcy highlight misunderstandings and growth as they challenge their prejudices to find genuine affection.

## Title: The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe
Four siblings enter the magical land of Narnia through a wardrobe and join the battle against the White Witch. Guided by the lion Aslan, they learn lessons of bravery, sacrifice, and faith in a story rich with allegory and adventure.

## Title: Dracula
Jonathan Harker travels to Transylvania and encounters Count Dracula, who soon sets his sights on London. As Dracula spreads his curse, a group of allies, led by Van Helsing, fights to stop him. The novel explores fear, seduction, and immortality.

## Title: Frankenstein
Victor Frankenstein creates a sentient creature in an experiment gone wrong. Rejected by society, the creature seeks vengeance and understanding. Shelley’s novel explores the consequences of unchecked ambition and the search for humanity.

## Title: The Picture of Dorian Gray
Dorian Gray remains physically youthful while a hidden portrait ages and reveals his moral decay. His pursuit of pleasure and beauty leads to corruption and despair in Oscar Wilde’s cautionary tale about vanity and conscience.

## Title: Les Misérables
Jean Valjean, a former convict, seeks redemption in post-revolutionary France. Pursued by the relentless Inspector Javert, he aids the poor and protects a young girl, Cosette. The story is one of justice, love, sacrifice, and social change.

## Title: The Little Prince
A pilot stranded in the desert meets a mysterious boy from another planet. Through poetic parables and encounters with odd characters, the story reflects on love, innocence, and the essence of what truly matters in life.
